We cannot be silenced.

The Philippines is a nation of democracy, and no law can get in the way of the power of its citizens. Here’s how you can speak your mind and influence the decisions of our legislators.

Be heard

0 Understand the issue

Read up! Here are some links to get you started on some research online.

  • The Cybercrime Bill This is the cybercrime law document (reformatted by bafox of reddit). This is also available from the gov.ph site here.
  • Google News compiles the list of cybercrime-related news articles as they happen.
  • Internetfreedom.ph has a list of senators who have voiced their intentions to amend the law, and those who haven’t yet.
  • PIFA.ph The Philippine Internet Freedom Alliance organizes efforts related to stopping the cyber martial law.
  • Reddit Reddit had a discussion on the cybercrime law, and includes a detailed lay-person analysis of its contents.

1 Send an email

Pour your thoughts out into a letter. Be as emotional as you need to be, but don’t make any personal attacks. Here’s a template to get you started. Make sure to change it up to add your thoughts in!

Dear Senator,

Great day! I’m writing to you about the recent issues about the online libel provision of of the cybercrime law.

Senator, I fear for the future of our country. I think the online libel provision can jeopardize our democracy. I want to live in a country where the citizens are free to praise the politicians, or point out their flaws, without fear of their words being taken against them.

Please, I urge you to support abolishing this provision from the law, for the sake of citizens like me and for our nation.

Yours truly,
Rico Sta. Cruz

Send your own message

2 Phone in your concerns

All senators have phone lines open to the public! Use them to speak up your concerns and ask for their support.

“Hello! Ako po si Rico Sta. Cruz ng Makati. Tumatawag po ako tungkol sa isyu ng online libel at ang cybercrime bill. Gusto ko po’ng malaman ang posisyon ni senador ukol dito, at magpahayag ng aking adhikain.”

“Hello! I’m Rico Sta. Cruz of Makati. I’m calling about the issue of online libel and the cybercrime bill. I’d like to learn about the senator’s position on this issue, and perhaps tell you about my stance on it for your consideration.”

Introduce yourself.

A member of the senator’s staff is going to answer your call. Be polite and don’t be shy! They take these kind of calls often. Feel free to speak in either English or Tagalog.

Ask who you can speak to about asking about the issue and your concerns.

“Ano po ang posisyon ni senador tungkol dito? Sya po ba ay su-suporta sa pag-apila ng batas na ito?”

“How does the senator feel about this issue? Will he/she be supporting the amendment of this law?”

Understand the issue.

Ask about the senator’s stance on the issue. It may be different from what you already know about.

“Sa tingin ko ay ang online libel provision ng cybercrime bill ay makaka-sira ng demokrasya ng ating lipunan. Bilang isang botante, ako po ay natatakot para sa kinabukasan ng ating bayan. Gusto ko po sanang mahingi ang suporta ni senador sa pag apila ng batas na ito, para sa mga mamayanang tulad ko at para sa ating bayan.”

“I think the online libel provision on the cybercrime law jeopardizes the democracy of the country as a whole. As a voter, I fear for the future of our country. I urge the senator to support abolishing this provision from the law, for the sake of citizens like me and for our nation.”

Speak your mind.

Ask for the support of the senator to support your cause. Present your arguments rationally. Refrain from making personal attacks; instead, be rational and stress why this issue is important to you.

Be sure to follow up!

If you call, send an email a few days later as well just to be sure that your call isn’t forgotten.

Write some letters

The senator’s offices have addresses you can write to. This can be more effective than an email.

To send mail, simply visit your nearest post office. You will need to buy a postage stamp (very cheap) to attach to your envelope.
